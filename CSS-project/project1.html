Physics Project

Project 1 was a year long accumulation of projects that was done in fulfillment of my Honors Engineering Physics I and II classes. These classes were taken during FALL 2015 and SPRING 2016. During the first semester, there were a total of 5 projects that were given to us students. While the second semester only 4 projects were given. Thus, the total projects within the two semesters were 9 projects. These projects were all done using Wolfram Mathematica 10.3 as requested by the professor.

The projects given in during these two semesters have primarily two goals in mind. Firstly, the professor intended to introduced students to a simple programming software. By learning a simple programming software, students are now able to solve problems involving more realistic parameters. By doing so, I learnt how to use the basics of Mathematica and to be able to visualize problems and solutions.

Challenges

There were many challenges that I faced during that year. One of the first challenges I faced was learning to code on MATLAB. That was the first time I was exposed to basic coding. It was a hard struggle for weeks before understanding the basics. By then the difficulty of the course had increased exponentially. Not only I had to learn how to code, I also had to learn the new course materials. Before any coding could be done, I would have to solve the problem by hand or at least draft out the layout of how I would solve the problem. With these challenges adding up one after another, I was able learn much more in these classes.
